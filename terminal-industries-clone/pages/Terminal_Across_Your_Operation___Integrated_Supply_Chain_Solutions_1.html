The Problem

Does your operation run on blind spots?

Fragmented Systems & Data Silos

Gate, yard, TMS, and WMS often don’t share data, causing blind spots and duplicated work.

Poor Real-Time Visibility

Operators can’t see inbound ETA changes, trailer locations, or dock availability across the network

High Detention & Demurrage Costs

Delays at any point (gate, yard, dock, warehouse) ripple through the chain and create financial penalties.

Lack of Predictive Planning

Inability to anticipate surges, disruptions, or carrier delays prevents proactive adjustments

The Solution

Terminal Solves Problems Across Your Logistics Operation, Differently

01A Digital Bridge Between TMS and WMS

Most platforms stop at yard visibility, leaving a huge gap between transportation and warehouse unsolved. Terminal uniquely connects over-the-road data from TMS with warehouse operations from WMS, giving supply chain leaders a single, connected view.

02A Single Pane of Glass for All Yards

Other systems manage sites one by one. Terminal delivers an enterprise-wide dashboard where all yards are connected, digitized, and automated. Managers can toggle across sites, compare performance, and standardize best practices — creating network-level efficiency.

03True AI-Native Architecture, Not AI Features

Competitors market AI as an add-on. Terminal is built ground-up as AI-native with computer vision at its core. That means unique capabilities like automated yard missions and anomaly alerts that others can’t replicate.

04End-to-end Orchestration, Not Task Optimization

Most YMS tools optimize one function at a time. Terminal coordinates every asset and workflow from gate to dock and back. If a truck is delayed, the system reroutes, reassigns, and alerts automatically — delivering orchestration competitors simply can’t match.

05Cloud-Ready Deployments, Not IT-Heavy Rollouts

Traditional systems require costly hardware and long implementations. Terminal runs on a cloud-based infrastructure with lightweight hardware, pre-configured bundles, and a low IT lift. Deployments happen in days, not quarters.
