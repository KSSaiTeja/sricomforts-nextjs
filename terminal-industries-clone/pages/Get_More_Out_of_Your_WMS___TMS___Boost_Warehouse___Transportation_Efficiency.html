Get More Out of WMS/TMS

Unlock the Value of Your Existing Logistics Systems

You’ve invested in a WMS to optimize your warehouse and a TMS to streamline transportation, but bottlenecks, yard delays, and frustrated carriers persist. The problem isn’t your systems, it’s the yard. The yard is where planned efficiency meets operational chaos, and without real-time visibility, it remains a blind spot.

The Problem

Traditional YMS Systems Aren’t Enough

Most traditional Yard Management Systems (YMS) focus on optimizing individual tasks rather than orchestrating the entire yard. They rely heavily on manual inputs and fragmented processes, leaving blind spots and delays that prevent your WMS and TMS from operating at full efficiency. Without real-time, end-to-end visibility, critical yard data—like trailer status, spotter movements, and dock availability—never reaches your warehouse or transportation systems. As a result, your investments in WMS and TMS are underutilized, and the yard remains a data black hole, and your operations at risk.

The Solution

Terminal is Your Yard’s Central Nervous System

Terminal Yard Operating SystemTM connects the "brain" of your TMS with the "body" of your WMS. With real-time gate-to-dock visibility, we track every asset and event, creating a single source of truth across your operation. Manual guesswork, phone calls, and spreadsheets are replaced with automation and clarity.

Unlocks Warehouse Value

Terminal ensures your WMS operates at peak efficiency by providing real-time yard data, enabling pre-planned labor and resources, and accelerating dock operations.

Inbound Asset Notifications

Trailer arrivals automatically update your WMS, allowing teams to stage labor and equipment for seamless receiving.

Spotting Task Management

Inventory Reconciliation

By providing real-time visibility into trailers, a YOS enables the WMS to conduct more accurate cycle counts and inventory reconciliations, reducing discrepancies and saving audit time.

Dynamic Receiving Prioritization

Terminal YOSTM can inform the WMS of high-priority inbound loads (e.g., just-in-time inventory), allowing the WMS to dynamically adjust receiving plans and allocate resources for faster turnaround.

Agentic Exception Handling

Our AI-enabled system will detect issues, diagnose root causes, and take intelligent corrective actions in real-time across all prescribed yard missions.

Unlocks Transportation Value

Terminal turns your yard into a strategic asset for TMS-driven operations, reducing detention fees, improving carrier relations, and enhancing fleet utilization.

Automated Trailer Status Updates

Real-time trailer status feeds directly to your TMS, creating a single source of truth.

Detention & Demurrage Alerts

Automatic alerts when dwell times exceed thresholds, preventing unnecessary costs.

Yard-Informed Trip Planning

TMS can make smarter carrier assignments and route decisions based on exact trailer locations, reducing empty miles and delays.

Carrier Performance Management

The YOSTM provides granular data on a carrier's yard turn times, enabling the TMS to generate performance reports and identify partners who consistently meet or exceed service level agreements.

Empty Trailer Visibility for Trip Creation

A YOSTM gives the TMS a real-time count and location of empty trailers in the yard, allowing it to automatically identify available assets for new outbound trips, reducing unnecessary repositioning and wait times.

The Most Common Technology Hurdles in the Yard

Here are the most common hurdles to technology adoption in the yard. Choose the one that is holding you back to see how Terminal can help.

Resistance to operational change

Yards traditionally rely on manual processes and are resistant to change. This ingrained culture makes it difficult to introduce new technology. Terminal's approach provides a strong ROI case to business leaders, making the financial benefits of change compelling, while its simple, turnkey deployment and managed infrastructure reduce the friction of adoption.

Worker resistance to automation

Workers worry about losing their jobs to new technology. This fear can lead to resistance from frontline staff and create a negative environment for adoption. Terminal uses a human-in-the-loop onboarding process that redefines roles, allowing staff to be redeployed to higher-value tasks, and provides ongoing support, which alleviates job security fears.

Skepticism from failed pilots

Customers are wary of new solutions due to past failures with other vendors. This long memory of past disappointments makes them cautious about investing again. Terminal builds trust with referenceable customers and proven performance, demonstrating a faster time-to-value (5 days vs. competitors' 5+ months) and lower deployment costs through rapid, successful results.

Fear of workflow disruption

Operators are afraid that new technology will break their existing, established, and profitable processes. This fear of risk outweighs the upside benefits for many companies. Terminal’s simple, turnkey deployment model and modular, on/off capability minimize this risk, allowing customers to start small and integrate the solution without a complete overhaul of their operations.

Cybersecurity and privacy risks

New interconnected yard systems introduce new vulnerabilities and data management risks. Companies with legacy systems or fragmented operations are especially vulnerable to these threats. As a fully managed solution with a unified, cloud-based platform, Terminal provides a secure, single-source system that is inherently more secure than managing a fragmented collection of different legacy tools.
